# Key Players for Aerospace Republic Research (1935-1955)

## Core Thesis
The period 1935-1955 saw American leaders create the "rules-based international order" through decisions that prioritized aerospace dominance, financial hegemony, and information control over industrial strength - creating contradictions that define our current crisis.

## Tier 1: Central Architects (Must Have Full Biographies)

### Franklin D. Roosevelt (1882-1945)
**Role**: Creator of the modern surveillance state and wartime censorship apparatus
**Key Decisions**: 
- Built FBI/OSS surveillance partnership
- Created wartime censorship regime
- Chose Europe-first strategy
- Suppressed dissent about Soviet intentions
**Best Biographies**:
1. *The Definitive FDR* by James MacGregor Burns (4 volumes)
2. *FDR* by Jean Edward Smith
3. *Franklin D. Roosevelt: A Political Life* by Robert Dallek
**Primary Sources**: FDR Library, Hopkins papers

### Douglas MacArthur (1880-1964)
**Role**: The suppressed alternative - Asia-first strategist
**Key Decisions**:
- Advocated Pacific/China priority
- Opposed limited war doctrine
- Warned against losing China
- Challenged civilian control
**Best Biographies**:
1. *American Caesar* by William Manchester
2. *MacArthur* by Arthur Herman
3. *The General vs. The President* by H.W. Brands
**Primary Sources**: MacArthur Memorial, RG 554 National Archives

### George C. Marshall (1880-1959)
**Role**: System builder who chose Europe over Asia
**Key Decisions**:
- Europe-first strategy
- Marshall Plan (deindustrialization seeds)
- Failed China mission
- Fired MacArthur
**Best Biographies**:
1. *George Marshall* (4 volumes) by Forrest Pogue
2. *The Mantle of Command* by Nigel Hamilton
3. *George Marshall: Defender of the Republic* by David L. Roll
**Primary Sources**: Marshall Foundation, VMI archives

### Dean Acheson (1893-1971)
**Role**: Architect of permanent confrontation
**Key Decisions**:
- NATO creation
- NSC-68 authorship
- Korea intervention design
- China containment policy
**Best Biographies**:
1. *Dean Acheson: A Life in the Cold War* by Robert L. Beisner
2. *Present at the Creation* (memoir)
3. *The Secretary* by John Lamberton Harper
**Primary Sources**: Truman Library, State Department FRUS

## Data Collection Strategy

### Priority Order
1. **Tier 1 first** - These are the core narrative drivers
2. **Best biography per person** - Start with the most cited/comprehensive
3. **Primary sources** - Letters, memos, reports from their papers
4. **Contemporary accounts** - Newspapers, magazines from 1935-1955
5. **Opposition voices** - What their critics said at the time

### Metadata to Extract
- Key decisions with dates
- Relationships/conflicts with other players
- Alternative paths not taken
- Quotes supporting/contradicting thesis
- Contemporary criticism

### Biographical Crossroads
Focus on moments where these figures intersected:
- 1940-41: FDR/Marshall vs isolationists
- 1945: Truman/Marshall/MacArthur on Japan occupation
- 1946: Acheson/Forrestal creating Cold War architecture  
- 1947: Marshall Plan debates
- 1950-51: MacArthur/Truman/Marshall confrontation
- 1953: Eisenhower inheriting the system
